About Zolia.

Made in Boostraps

Zolia Revolves Entirely Around The Concept Of Open-Fire Cooking.

With a minimalistic interior that accentuates the heritage building it occupies, resembling a hearth. Spanning three levels and featuring distinct areas—the main dining room, a wine bar, and a rooftop bar—the main dining room offers a view of the open kitchen equipped with two charcoal pits (one for seafood and starters, the other for meat) and a wood-fired oven.

Zolia Focuses Its Menu On The Imaginative Use Of Wood Fire, Eliminating Electricity And Gas For A Back-To-Basics Cooking Style.

Cooking Only On Open Flames.

Even the inside, kept simple to emphasize the historical building it is housed in, is hearth-like.

The menu at Zolia centers around the innovative use of wood flame, without electricity or gas.
